## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: The angle-brief generator always appends

`/ssc.ads-brief` (`ssc-ads-brief`) SHALL propose NEW angles on **every** invocation, including when the idea already carries briefs. The produce-once guard — the rule that STOPs and writes nothing when `list_briefs` returns ≥1 brief — SHALL be removed, and no equivalent refusal SHALL replace it.

Regenerating an idea's angles is what appending **is**. The skill SHALL NOT offer, require, or instruct any "discard everything and re-produce" path, and SHALL NOT expose a force/regenerate mode. An angle the operator does not want is an angle they never approve (and may discard deliberately in the dashboard); it is never something the skill destroys on their behalf.

Cold start is unchanged: on an idea with no briefs the skill proposes up to five angles, self-scores them, drops and regenerates any rated ≤3, and saves the passing set as DRAFT briefs.

#### Scenario: Re-invoked on an idea that already has briefs

- **WHEN** `/ssc.ads-brief <idea_id>` is invoked for an idea whose `list_briefs` returns five briefs
- **THEN** the skill does NOT stop, and it proposes only angles that are distinct from all five, saving each passing angle as a NEW DRAFT brief alongside the existing rows

#### Scenario: Adding a sixth angle to a five-angle idea

- **WHEN** the operator wants one further angle on a concept that already carries five approved briefs
- **THEN** re-invoking `/ssc.ads-brief <idea_id>` is the whole procedure — no brief is discarded, no brief is edited, and the five existing briefs (and the copy and creatives anchored to them) are untouched

#### Scenario: Cold start is unchanged

- **WHEN** `/ssc.ads-brief <idea_id>` is invoked for an idea with no briefs
- **THEN** the skill proposes up to five angles, drops and regenerates any rated ≤3 until every angle is ≥4, and saves the passing set as DRAFT briefs — exactly as before this change

### Requirement: Existing briefs form the taken set

Before proposing anything, the skill SHALL read **ALL** of the idea's existing briefs via `list_briefs` (**any status** — draft and approved alike) and SHALL treat their angles as the **taken set**: each brief's `angle_label` **and** its five narrative fields (`hook_direction`, `core_message`, `why_now`, `story_moment`, `cta`).

A draft brief the operator has not approved is still taken — it is an angle they have already been shown and not chosen. Re-proposing it MUST NOT happen.

#### Scenario: Drafts count as taken, not only approved briefs

- **WHEN** an idea carries three approved briefs and two draft briefs
- **THEN** all five are read as the taken set, and no proposed angle duplicates the anchor of any of them — including the two drafts

#### Scenario: The full narrative is the comparison basis

- **WHEN** the skill compares a candidate angle against an existing brief
- **THEN** it compares against that brief's five narrative fields as well as its `angle_label` — a candidate that carries a different label but the same anchor and the same argument is a duplicate and is rejected

### Requirement: Proposed angles are distinct from the taken set and from each other

Every angle the skill proposes SHALL be genuinely DISTINCT from **every existing brief** on the idea AND from every other angle in the same batch. The distinctiveness rule already applied within one batch — each angle anchors a **different** persona trigger point / objection / myth, expressed through the concept — SHALL be extended across the whole existing set.

The self-score's distinctiveness criterion SHALL be assessed against the taken set plus the current batch, not against the current batch alone. A candidate that near-duplicates an existing brief SHALL score low on distinctiveness and be dropped or regenerated by the existing quality loop; no angle rated ≤3 is ever saved.

#### Scenario: A candidate that re-uses a taken anchor is rejected

- **WHEN** a candidate angle anchors on a persona objection that an existing brief already anchors on
- **THEN** it scores low on distinctiveness and is dropped and regenerated — never saved, even though it is distinct from the other candidates in the same batch

#### Scenario: Distinctiveness holds within the batch as well

- **WHEN** two candidate angles in the same batch anchor on the same trigger point
- **THEN** at most one survives — the within-batch rule is unchanged and is applied together with the across-set rule

### Requirement: The skill never pads the angle set

The skill SHALL NOT invent a near-duplicate angle to produce a non-empty result. If the concept, the ad-set `build_spec`, and the persona detail doc genuinely support **no further distinct angle**, the skill SHALL say so plainly and SHALL write **nothing**.

Writing nothing on an already-populated idea is an **ordinary, expected outcome**, not an error and not a degenerate case — an idea that has already yielded its distinct angles will often support no further one. The skill SHALL report it as a clean result naming the reason (the concept's distinct angles are exhausted) and SHALL NOT relax the distinctiveness bar to avoid it.

#### Scenario: Angle supply exhausted

- **WHEN** the idea's five existing briefs already cover every distinct trigger point, objection, and myth the concept + persona doc support
- **THEN** the skill saves no brief, reports plainly that the concept supports no further distinct angle, and suggests sharpening the concept (title / tags / ad_notes) if more angles are genuinely wanted

#### Scenario: A padded angle is worse than an empty result

- **WHEN** the skill can only reach a non-empty result by proposing an angle that near-duplicates an existing brief
- **THEN** it writes nothing — a duplicate angle is a wasted production track (its own copy run, its own creative chain) and a curation trap, so an honest empty result is the correct outcome

### Requirement: Existing briefs are read-only input to the skill

The skill SHALL NEVER modify an existing brief: no delete, no edit, no re-write, no re-score, no `angle_label` change, no status change. Existing briefs — draft **and** approved — are read-only input.

The tools `delete` and `edit` MUST NOT appear in the skill's frontmatter `tools:` list. The propose-only invariant SHALL be structural, not merely instructed: the skill's only write to the angle set is `save_brief`, which mints DRAFT briefs and takes no `status` argument. The skill SHALL NEVER call `approve`.

#### Scenario: An existing brief is never touched

- **WHEN** the skill appends new angles to an idea carrying approved briefs that already anchor copy and creatives
- **THEN** every existing brief row is left byte-for-byte unchanged — its `angle_label`, its narrative fields, its `score`, its `comment`, and its `status` — and the copy and creatives anchored to it are unaffected

#### Scenario: The skill holds no destructive tool

- **WHEN** the skill's frontmatter `tools:` list is read
- **THEN** it contains neither `delete` nor `edit`, so destroying or mutating a brief is not merely forbidden by prose but unreachable

#### Scenario: Appended angles are drafts, never approvals

- **WHEN** the skill saves an appended angle
- **THEN** it calls `save_brief`, the row is created as `draft`, and the skill calls no approval tool — the operator approves the angles worth producing in `/ad/[month]/[id]`

### Requirement: The skill states what discarding an angle actually costs

The "discard ALL briefs in the dashboard first, then re-invoke" remediation SHALL be **removed** from the skill and the command — the operation it describes is no longer how new angles are obtained.

In its place, the skill SHALL state accurately what deleting an angle means, so an operator who *does* want to discard one knows the cost: the brief is **HARD-deleted** (the `briefs` table has no tombstone — the angle is gone for good, and cannot be restored), and the deletion takes that angle's produced work with it — its copy and its creatives. The statement SHALL describe the server's **actual current** semantics at the time the skill ships; it SHALL NOT describe the deletion as a routine reset, and SHALL NOT recommend it.

#### Scenario: The discard-to-regenerate instruction is gone

- **WHEN** `ssc-ads-brief/SKILL.md` and `commands/ssc.ads-brief.md` are read
- **THEN** neither instructs the operator to discard briefs in order to regenerate — appending is the regeneration path, and no text describes deletion as the way to get new angles

#### Scenario: Deletion is described by its blast radius

- **WHEN** the skill mentions discarding an angle
- **THEN** it says the brief is hard-deleted with no tombstone and that the angle's copy and creatives go with it — the cost is named, not softened

### Requirement: Deleting an approved brief is refused

`delete(entity='brief')` SHALL continue to REFUSE while the brief is `approved`, returning `brief_approved` and writing nothing; the operator MUST un-approve it first. *(Server requirement — BrandOS MCP server; not implementable in this repo, which ships only markdown skills and one governance hook.)*

Un-approving is a demotion, which requires the **`approve`** capability. Agents hold `edit` and never `approve`, so an agent can never un-approve a brief and therefore can never reach the destructive path at all. This gate SHALL NOT be relaxed by any other requirement in this capability.

#### Scenario: Approved brief cannot be deleted

- **WHEN** `delete(entity='brief', id=B)` is called and `B.status = 'approved'`
- **THEN** the call is refused with `brief_approved`, nothing is written, no media is purged, and no content row is removed

#### Scenario: An agent cannot reach the destructive path

- **WHEN** a propose-only agent (holding `edit`, never `approve`) attempts to un-approve a brief in order to delete it
- **THEN** the un-approving `edit` is denied for want of the `approve` capability, so the brief stays approved and the delete stays refused

### Requirement: Deleting an unapproved brief cascades to its dependents

Deleting an **unapproved** brief SHALL succeed and SHALL take its dependents with it; the current `brief_has_creatives` refusal — which tells the operator to hand-delete each visual layer first — SHALL be REPLACED by this cascade. *(Server requirement — BrandOS MCP server; not implementable in this repo.)*

- The brief's `creatives` SHALL be HARD-deleted, each purging its media on the Go asset service (irreversible, cross-service).
- The brief's `content` rows — copy, headline, description, image_content: everything written **from** that angle — SHALL be **HARD-deleted**. This REPLACES today's `content.brief_id ON DELETE SET NULL` unbind. An orphaned copy row that can never be attributed to an angle is precisely the input that produces wrong-angle grounding downstream, so the cascade SHALL remove it rather than strand it.
- The whole cascade SHALL be **ONE transaction**, individually audited per destroyed row, and SHALL refuse **atomically** — any guard failure writes nothing at all.

#### Scenario: A brief with creatives deletes cleanly

- **WHEN** an unapproved brief `B` with an approved background, an approved model, and a draft composite is deleted by a caller holding the required capability
- **THEN** all of `B`'s creatives are hard-deleted with their media purged, `B` is removed, and the operator is NOT told to delete the visual layers by hand first

#### Scenario: Content written from the angle is destroyed, not orphaned

- **WHEN** an unapproved brief `B` with four approved `copy` rows and two `headline` rows is deleted
- **THEN** those six `content` rows are hard-deleted along with `B` — they are NOT unbound with `brief_id → NULL` and left behind as rows no consumer can attribute to an angle

#### Scenario: The cascade is atomic

- **WHEN** any part of the cascade fails a guard
- **THEN** nothing is written: no brief removed, no content row deleted, no media purged — the delete refuses as a whole

### Requirement: Delete capability escalates with the blast radius

The capability required to delete a brief SHALL depend on what the delete would destroy. *(Server requirement — BrandOS MCP server; not implementable in this repo.)*

- A brief with **NO dependents** (no creatives, no content) MAY be deleted with the **`edit`** capability — nothing produced is lost, and the angle is cheaply re-obtained by re-invoking `/ssc.ads-brief`.
- A brief **WITH dependents** SHALL require the **`approve`** capability. An `edit`-only caller attempting a cascading delete SHALL be refused with `forbidden`, writing nothing.

This applies the server's own established principle — *destroying is a stronger move than demoting, so it must not be reachable at a weaker capability* — to the cascade's blast radius. It is what keeps a propose-only agent **structurally** unable to destroy produced work, rather than merely instructed not to.

#### Scenario: Dependent-free brief deletes at edit

- **WHEN** an `edit`-holding caller deletes an unapproved brief that has no creatives and no content
- **THEN** the delete succeeds — the angle is discarded and nothing produced is lost

#### Scenario: Cascading delete is refused at edit

- **WHEN** an `edit`-holding caller (for example any agent) attempts to delete an unapproved brief that has creatives or content
- **THEN** the call is refused with `forbidden` and writes nothing — no row removed, no media purged

#### Scenario: Cascading delete succeeds at approve

- **WHEN** an operator holding the `approve` capability deletes an unapproved brief that has creatives and content
- **THEN** the cascade runs and the brief, its creatives (media purged), and its content rows are destroyed in one audited transaction

### Requirement: A brief whose work is live-scheduled cannot be deleted

If **any** of the brief's `content` rows is referenced by a **live schedule entry**, the whole delete SHALL be REFUSED and nothing SHALL be written — deleting an angle MUST NOT be able to silently unschedule a running ad. *(Server requirement — BrandOS MCP server; not implementable in this repo.)*

This mirrors the existing `content` delete guard (a content row is already refused while a live schedule row references it); the cascade MUST NOT become a way to route around it.

#### Scenario: Live schedule blocks the whole cascade

- **WHEN** a brief has ten content rows and exactly one of them is referenced by a live schedule entry
- **THEN** the delete is refused entirely — the brief survives, all ten content rows survive, every creative survives, and no media is purged

#### Scenario: The cascade cannot route around the content guard

- **WHEN** a caller cannot delete a live-scheduled content row directly (it is refused)
- **THEN** they also cannot delete it indirectly by deleting its brief — the brief delete applies the same refusal

### Requirement: The delete reports its blast radius before destroying anything

The brief delete SHALL be able to report **what it WOULD destroy** — counts of creatives, of images purged, and of content rows — **before** anything is destroyed, so the dashboard can show the operator the blast radius ahead of an irreversible act. *(Server requirement — BrandOS MCP server; not implementable in this repo.)*

Whether this is a `dry_run` flag on `delete` or a separate preflight read is the server's call. The requirement is that the counts are obtainable **before** the destruction, not merely recoverable from the audit log afterwards.

#### Scenario: Operator sees the cost before confirming

- **WHEN** the operator initiates the discard of an angle carrying four creatives and twelve content rows
- **THEN** the surface can report those counts (4 creatives / 4 images purged / 12 content rows) before the delete runs, and nothing is destroyed by the reporting itself

#### Scenario: Preflight destroys nothing

- **WHEN** the blast-radius report is requested for a brief
- **THEN** no row is removed, no media is purged, and no audit entry for a destruction is written — the report is read-only
