## User Input

```text
$ARGUMENTS
```

Consider the user input above before proceeding (if not empty). Expected inputs — **both required**:

- **Ad concept idea ID** (`idea_id`) — the id of ONE approved ad concept (an `ideas` row, `channel='ad'`, `status='approved'`).
- **Angle brief ID** (`brief_id`) — the id of the operator's **chosen approved angle brief** for this concept (produced first by `/ssc.ads-brief`, approved in the dashboard). **Every read and every generate call keys on it.**

If `idea_id` or `brief_id` is missing, ask the operator for it (one question) before dispatching — do not invent one. There is **no `date` selector**: a `brief_id` is idea-scoped, so the producer always takes an explicit `idea_id`.

Optional (passed through unchanged — this command **never resolves or chooses** a chain, subject, or product itself):

- **Stage** (`stage: subject` | `stage: text`) — explicitly select an OPTIONAL or terminal step: **Subject** (Step 1 — a standalone person) or **Text** (Step 4 — render the approved headline over the chain tip). Omit it and the skill works the **default flow** (the Full-image chain, plus an Edit when `edit:` is given).
- **Edit** (`edit: <what to change>`) — request **Step 3 (Edit)** over the selected chain's tip (e.g. *"đặt sản phẩm lên bàn bên trái"*, *"ánh sáng ấm hơn"*). Product placement is one kind of edit; the step is **optional and repeatable** (re-run with another `edit:` for edit-on-edit).
- **Chain** (`chain: <background_creative_id>`) — a brief carries **N parallel visual chains**, each rooted at one **approved `background` (Full image)** creative. This names the chain to advance. Not needed with 0 approved Full images (Step 2 is chain creation) or exactly 1 (unambiguous). With **≥2** and no `chain`, the skill **stops and asks** which one.
- **New chain** (`new-chain`) — mint an **ADDITIONAL** chain: 3 fresh Full-image candidates **even though the brief already has an approved Full image**. The **only** way Step 2 runs once a root exists — so a plain re-run never burns credits on new roots. `chain` + `new-chain` together is contradictory and stops.
- **Subject** (`subject: <creative_id>`) — which **approved subject** anchors the Full image. Only needed with **more than one** approved subject (with one, auto; with none, the Full image is a from-scratch text-to-image).
- **Product** (`product: <creative_id>`) — which **approved brief-level product** anchors the Full image (or an Edit). Only needed with **more than one** approved product.
- **Revision note** (`revise: <note>`) — a free-text correction for the **active step of the selected chain**. It makes the skill **rewrite** that step's prompt and generate ONE fresh candidate. Without it, a step with a pending draft in that chain simply stops.
- **Model** (`model`) — a fal model id. Omit it and the server role default governs (the Full-image role auto-resolves from attached refs).
- **Period** (`period`, `YYYY-MM`) — informational only; the month the concept belongs to, used when pointing the operator at `/ad/[month]/[id]`.

**Removed inputs** — if the operator supplies them, say so and drop them: `layout_hint` / `hard_paste` no longer exist (`compose_ad_visual` is now a generic edit — placement direction goes in the `edit:` change text). `uploaded_media_id` / `uploaded_media_ref` no longer exist (a real model photo is placed via a dashboard upload). There is likewise **no `image_content_id`** and **no `n`**.

This command is the **visual-production half** of the Ads pipeline — the **credit-spending** counterpart of the propose-only ImageStudio prompt authors (`/ssc.image-prompt`). Its **three preconditions**, checked in order, are: an **approved ad concept**, an **approved angle brief** for it, and **≥1 approved `copy`** for that brief (`/ssc.ads-produce <brief_id> copy` produces the copy). **Step 4 Text additionally needs an approved `image_content` headline** (`/ssc.ads-produce <brief_id> image_content`); `image_content` is otherwise **never read or sized into** a Subject / Full-image / Edit scene prompt. The producer operates **per concept**, **per angle (brief)**, **per chain**, and **per step**, never on a whole plan: it reads **no** `channel_plan` gate flags. **Phase 1 wires only the ad channel** — a non-ad idea stops cleanly.

## What to do

This command is a thin entry point — it holds **no** orchestration logic. It dispatches the single visual producer **`ssc-image`** (`idea_id`, `brief_id`, optional `stage` / `edit` / `chain` / `new-chain` / `subject` / `product` / `revise` / `model` / `period` passthrough) for the resolved concept, then stops. **It does not resolve chains, does not pick one, and does not pick a subject or product** — that is the skill's job, and the skill stops and asks rather than guessing.

`ssc-image` is a **state-driven, per-step stepper with CHAIN-SCOPED state** that **spends fal credits** by calling the generate tools. On each invocation it reads that **brief's** creatives, groups them into **chains by the `derived_from` lineage every row already carries** (an **approved `background` (Full image)** is a chain **ROOT** and its id IS the chain id; a `composite` (Edit) and a `text` derive from the chain tip; `subject` and `product` are **brief-level** anchors shared by every chain; the old `model`/Scene key is dormant), **selects ONE chain (or the brief-level Subject/Full-image batch)**, works the **single next open step** — **`subject` (opt) → `background` "Full image" → `composite` "Edit" (opt, repeatable) → `text`** — saves DRAFT creative(s), and **stops**.

| The skill does | Then the operator… |
|---|---|
| Checks the three preconditions in order — approved ad concept → `brief_id` is an **approved** angle brief of it → **≥1 approved `copy`** for that brief (stopping with the exact unmet precondition otherwise). Resolves the brief's creatives into **chains**, **selects ONE** (stopping to **ask** when ≥2 chains and no `chain`), and works the next open step **of that chain** — **authoring the full scene prompt itself** (it reaches the image engine verbatim), grounded in the brief's angle + the approved copy (a **meaning** source — its words are never named) + the persona detail doc + the brand/visual + compliance KB: **Subject** (`stage: subject` — a standalone person, an anchor candidate), **Full image** (chain creation — 3 distinct readings; **dual-role**: attaches the selected subject and/or product refs → Kontext image-edit built around them, or NEITHER → FLUX.2 text-to-image from scratch; approving one mints a chain root), **Edit** (`edit: <change>` — a generic repeatable prompt-to-edit over the chain tip), **Text** (`stage: text` — renders the approved headline over the chain tip). Saves DRAFT creatives and **stops**, naming **which chain it advanced** and how many chains the brief now has. Each step derives its state from what exists — skip Subject → the Full image is a text-to-image root; skip Edit → Text hangs off the Full image. | Opens `/ad/[month]/[id]`, **reviews / approves** (or **discards**) the saved DRAFT creative(s) for that step, then **re-runs `/ssc.image <idea_id> <brief_id> chain: <root id>`** — the skill detects the newly-approved step of that chain and works the next one. If a pending draft misses the mark, re-runs with **`revise: <note>`**. For a repeatable Edit, re-runs with **`edit: <change>`**. To start another parallel chain, re-runs with **`new-chain`**. |

**The Scene step is RETIRED.** The studio went from 5 steps to 4 — the standalone **Scene** step (backend layer `model`, tool `generate_model`) that composed a subject into a background is **gone**; compositing folds into the dual-role **Full image**. The skill **never calls `generate_model`** and never steps through `model` (the key stays dormant; old rows still read).

**A brief holds N parallel chains, and the skill never guesses which one.** With **≥2** approved Full images and no `chain`, it **stops and lists every chain** (root Full-image creative id, a one-line gist of that root's prompt, and how far the chain has got) and asks which to advance — it never picks by any heuristic, and it generates nothing on that run. An invalid `chain` / `subject` / `product` / `stage` stops the same way, naming the valid ones. Each generate call spends real credits, so an extra question is always cheaper than a wrong image.

**Don't like a pending draft? Re-run with `revise: <note>`.** The skill then **rewrites that step's prompt — of the selected chain** — applying the note and issues ONE fresh generate call; never a blind re-roll, never a second batch.

**This flow bakes no text into any upstream visual** — every Subject / Full-image / Edit prompt keeps a clean scene by describing it **positively**, never by naming the ad copy and **never with a reserved empty zone** (the old reserved text/subject-zone geometry is deleted). The words appear only at the **Text** step. The producer works **one step of one chain per run**, reads **no** channel_plan gates, and **saves DRAFT creatives immediately** (no in-chat candidate presentation, no in-chat revise loop). If the active step of the selected chain already has an unapproved DRAFT and no `revise` note was given, the skill stops and asks the operator to approve/discard it first — no second batch **in that chain**.

**A CHAIN completes; the BRIEF never does.** A chain is complete when it has an **approved `text`** (the finished ad visual with the headline rendered). The skill then stops, names **that chain**, reports how many chains the brief now has, and offers the two real next actions — start another chain with **`new-chain`**, or pick the **hero image** in the dashboard. It never reports the *brief* complete, and it never sets the cover.

## Governance

Nothing auto-approves, auto-applies, auto-discards, sets a cover, or auto-publishes. `ssc-image` **saves DRAFT `creative` rows** and stops; the operator reviews / approves / discards each step on the `/ad/[month]/[id]` page. **"Save" persists drafts; it is NOT approval** — it never flips a gate, and on the `background` (Full image) step **approving is also what curates a candidate into a chain ROOT**. Propose-only on approval (hard rule): the producer never calls `approve` (the ONLY gated promotion, denied to agents by the approval hook), never uses `edit` to demote / unapprove / **discard** a creative, never `save_creative_prompt`, **no `set_cover`** (which finished visual is the hero image is the operator's dashboard choice — not in the skill's tools), no `reorder_gallery`, no publish, no `update_budget` — it never uploads the product, and never bakes ad text into an upstream visual. It **never guesses a chain, subject, or product**: ambiguity is a STOP-and-ask, and it **never edits or renders text across chains** (it passes **this chain's** tip as `from`). The skill talks **only** to BrandOS MCP tools on the `ssc` surface — **never** a third-party image API, not even when a BrandOS call fails. **Each generate call spends fal credits**, so every unmet precondition, ambiguity, or server error is a clean STOP with the exact next action, never a retry-around or a silently-degraded run. All operator-facing prose and persisted notes are **Vietnamese** (image-model prompts are free-form — usually English renders best). Producing requires `edit` (plus `view` for the resolve/precondition reads); approving a draft later requires `approve` on the page.

## After it runs

After the skill saves a step's DRAFT creative(s) — its summary **names the chain it advanced** (the root Full-image creative id) and how many chains the brief now has — point the operator to `/ad/[month]/[id]` for the concept to **review / approve** the active step (`subject` → `background` "Full image" → `composite` "Edit" → `text`) — or **discard** it, or re-run with `revise: <note>` for a re-authored prompt — then **re-run this command** for the same `idea_id` + `brief_id`, passing **`chain: <root id>`** when the brief has more than one chain, to work that chain's next step. Approving a **Full image** is what **mints a chain root**.

- To build a **subject-anchored** scene: run `stage: subject`, approve a subject, then re-run (the Full image auto-attaches the approved subject; the role becomes a Kontext image-edit built around it). Skip Subject entirely and the Full image is a from-scratch text-to-image.
- The `product` anchor is the operator's own: they upload the **real** packaging photo and approve it in the dashboard (it is **brief-level** — one approved product serves every chain), then re-run.
- **Edit** is optional and repeatable: re-run with `edit: <change>` (over the chain tip), including product placement — no dedicated product step remains.
- **Text** (`stage: text`) renders the approved headline; it needs an approved `image_content` (`/ssc.ads-produce <brief_id> image_content`).

When a chain has an **approved `text`** it is **complete** — but the **brief is never "complete"**: start another parallel chain any time with **`/ssc.image <idea_id> <brief_id> new-chain`** (3 fresh Full-image candidates → approve one → a new root), and pick the **hero image** among the approved visuals in the dashboard (`set_cover` is yours, not the skill's). To work a **different angle**, approve a different brief (`/ssc.ads-brief <idea_id>`), get an approved `copy` for it via `/ssc.ads-produce <brief_id> copy`, and pass that `brief_id`.
